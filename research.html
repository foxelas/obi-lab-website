Research Themes

Topics and focus

Research on ensuring the safety of social information infrastructure

We conduct research to ensure the safety of social information infrastructure. In order to enable the safe distribution of information such as medical information, it is extremely important to verify the identity and control access, and to ensure interoperability between information systems by standardizing the functions of devices. In our laboratory, we investigate how to safely use various devices on the network while fully optimizing the performance, for example using devices with authentication function via a multifunctional IC chip. We are conducting research aimed at establishing the framework necessary for the safe use of various devices on networks.

In addition, with the aim of realizing the ideal form of the next-generation e-government, we are participating in the Solution Research Organization / Advanced Research Center for Social Information Science And Technology established at our university and conducting joint research. Here, we are conducting research and efforts toward the realization of public systems that enable people to directly and actively utilize their various information, such as the My Number system. For example, Fig. 3 shows a schematic diagram for confirming insurance qualifications using the Basic Resident Register Card at a medical institution. To achieve this, examination of the specifications of public IC cards is required, along with examination of the my number system for identifying individuals in the medical field, etc. We are developing various technologies that are directly related to people's lives.

Research on medical bioimaging

Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and X-ray CT occupy an important position in diagnosis as a technique for imaging information inside the human body from data collected outside the body. In our laboratory, we are developing a method to accurately collect the structure and function of the body as an image for the purpose of enabling early detection of diseases and highly accurate image diagnosis. The internal distribution image (reconstruction image) of the physical quantity targeted by these medical imaging devices is estimated from the observation data using the image reconstruction method. Conventional image reconstruction methods may not be able to fully utilize the observed information due to various restrictions of the observation system. In our laboratory, we have developed a method to accurately describe the characteristics of the observation system and perform statistical image reconstruction, and apply it to various objects.

A specific theme is the estimation of dynamic function images that represent the functional information of living organisms, which is one of the targets for using PET devices. Since the dynamic functional image is estimated using the plasma time activity curve (plasma Time Activity Curve) and the tissue time activity curve (tTAC), the estimated accuracy of the functional image. Is known to be highly dependent on the accuracy of tTAC. We are currently developing a four-dimensional reconstruction method based on DRAMA (Dynamic Row Action Maximum-Likelihood Algorithm) reconstruction as a method for estimating tTAC faster and with higher accuracy.
